TRAVEL HINDI

Hindi can make travel in India feel more connected

You do not need to become fluent before travelling to India. Even a small amount of Hindi can help you greet people warmly, ask simple questions, understand everyday words and feel more confident in places where English is not always common.

Private lessons can focus on the situations you are most likely to meet on your trip, whether you are visiting family, exploring markets, travelling between cities, joining a cultural tour or simply wanting to feel less dependent on translation apps.

PRACTICAL TRAVEL SITUATIONS

What travel Hindi lessons can help with

Lessons can be shaped around where you are going, who you are visiting and how much Hindi you already know.

👋

Greetings and introductions

Learn polite ways to say hello, introduce yourself, thank people and begin simple conversations.

🛒

Markets and shopping

Practise useful words for prices, quantities, directions, colours and everyday market conversations.

🚌

Transport and directions

Build confidence asking where something is, checking destinations and understanding common travel words.

🍲

Food and hospitality

Learn phrases for ordering food, discussing preferences and responding warmly when invited into homes.

🙏

Culture and respect

Use Hindi to show care in temples, family settings, community events and everyday cultural exchanges.

💬

Pronunciation practice

Practise saying common phrases aloud so they feel natural before you need them in real situations.

TRAVEL QUESTIONS

Before you travel

How soon should I start?

Starting several weeks before your trip gives you time to practise, but even a shorter preparation period can help with useful phrases and pronunciation.

Will I be fluent before travelling?

Fluency takes time. The goal for travel lessons is practical confidence: greetings, questions, common words and cultural awareness.

Can lessons match my itinerary?

Yes. You can discuss where you are going and what situations matter most, then shape lessons around your travel needs.
